RISK DISCLOSURE STATEMENT

Last Updated: January 15, 2025

IMPORTANT NOTICE

Real estate investing carries significant risks. You may lose some or all of your invested capital.

This Risk Disclosure Statement describes the principal risks of investing through BlockVest. You should read this carefully before investing.

By investing through BlockVest, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and accepted these risks.

1. GENERAL INVESTMENT RISKS

1.1 Loss of Capital

You may lose some or all of your investment. Real estate values fluctuate, and there is no guarantee you will recover your initial investment.

1.2 Past Performance

Past performance of properties, markets, or BlockVest's platform does not guarantee future results. Historical returns are not predictive.

1.3 No Guaranteed Returns

Projected returns are estimates based on assumptions. Actual returns may be significantly lower or negative.

1.4 Long-Term Investment

Real estate is illiquid. You should expect to hold investments for 3-10 years minimum. Even though we come in to solve the problem of illiquidity by giving access to your investment capital even immediately after ivestment. Please Do Note That: Premature Exits from investments might cost you some of your investment capital due to closing costs and other clearly stated fees to you.

2. MARKET RISKS

2.1 Economic Downturn

Economic recessions can cause:

  • • Property value declines
  • • Reduced rental demand
  • • Tenant payment defaults
  • • Difficulty selling properties

2.2 Real Estate Market Risks

Property markets are cyclical and can experience:

  • • Price corrections
  • • Oversupply (reducing rents and values)
  • • Reduced buyer demand
  • • Extended sales periods

2.3 Location-Specific Risks

Individual neighborhoods can decline due to:

  • • Crime increases
  • • Infrastructure deterioration
  • • Business closures
  • • Population shifts
  • • Environmental issues

2.4 Regulatory Changes

Government actions can affect property values:

  • • Tax increases
  • • New regulations restricting development
  • • Zoning changes
  • • Rent control policies
  • • Property acquisition for public use

3. PROPERTY-SPECIFIC RISKS

3.1 Vacancy Risk

Properties may experience periods without tenants, resulting in:

  • • Zero rental income
  • • Ongoing maintenance costs
  • • Market rent declines
  • • Tenant turnover expenses

3.2 Tenant Default

Tenants may fail to pay rent, requiring:

  • • Legal eviction processes (3-6 months)
  • • Lost income during vacancy
  • • Property damage repair costs
  • • Legal fees

3.3 Property Damage

Properties can be damaged by:

  • • Natural disasters (flooding, storms)
  • • Fire
  • • Vandalism
  • • Structural failures
  • • Tenant negligence

While properties are insured, coverage may not be complete, and claims take time to process.

3.4 Maintenance and Repair

Unexpected major repairs can occur:

  • • Roof replacement
  • • Plumbing/electrical system failures
  • • Foundation issues
  • • Environmental remediation

These can significantly reduce returns or require additional capital contributions.

3.5 Title and Legal Risks

Despite due diligence, properties may face:

  • • Ownership disputes
  • • Undisclosed liens or encumbrances
  • • Survey or boundary errors
  • • Zoning violations
  • • Land acquisition by government

4. INCOME AND RETURN RISKS

4.1 No Guaranteed Income

Dividend payments depend on:

  • • Property occupancy
  • • Tenant payment
  • • Operating expenses
  • • Property management performance

Dividends may be reduced, delayed, or eliminated.

4.2 Variable Returns

Actual returns may differ from projections due to:

  • • Market rent changes
  • • Expense increases
  • • Vacancy periods
  • • Property value changes

4.3 Inflation Risk

Inflation erodes purchasing power. While real estate generally tracks inflation, there is no guarantee your returns will keep pace.

4.4 Currency Risk

For dollar-denominated investments or international investors, naira devaluation affects real returns.

5. LIQUIDITY RISKS

5.1 Sale Below Purchase Price

When you exit, you may receive less than your initial investment due to:

  • • Property value decline
  • • Market conditions
  • • Sales costs and fees

6. PLATFORM AND OPERATIONAL RISKS

6.1 Platform Dependence

Your investment DOES NOT depend on BlockVest's continued operation. If BlockVest ceases operations:

  • • You retain property ownership with options to hire a new manager, sell or transfer ownership
  • • Property management may change
  • • Platform access may be limited
  • • Administrative costs may change

6.2 Property Management Risk

Property performance depends on management quality. Poor management can lead to:

  • • Reduced rental income
  • • Property deterioration
  • • Tenant dissatisfaction
  • • Increased expenses

6.3 Technology Risks

Platform disruptions from:

  • • System failures
  • • Cybersecurity breaches
  • • Data loss
  • • Service provider outages

May affect access to information or services.

6.4 Third-Party Dependencies

We rely on third parties for:

  • • Payment processing
  • • Property management
  • • Legal services
  • • Insurance coverage

Their failures can affect your investment.

7. REGULATORY AND LEGAL RISKS

7.1 Regulatory Changes

Changes in securities, real estate, or tax laws may:

  • • Affect investment structure
  • • Increase costs
  • • Limit investment options
  • • Change tax treatment

7.2 Compliance Risks

If BlockVest fails to maintain regulatory compliance:

  • • Platform may be suspended
  • • Penalties may be incurred
  • • Reputational damage may occur

7.3 Legal Disputes

Properties may become subject to:

  • • Tenant lawsuits
  • • Neighbor disputes
  • • Environmental claims
  • • Contract disputes

Legal costs and outcomes are unpredictable.

8. TAX RISKS

8.1 Tax Treatment

Tax laws are complex and subject to change. Your tax obligations depend on:

  • • Income level
  • • Other investments
  • • Changes in tax law
  • • Jurisdictional rules (for international investors)

8.2 Tax Liability

You are responsible for:

  • • Reporting investment income
  • • Paying applicable taxes
  • • Understanding tax obligations
  • • Consulting tax advisors

BlockVest withholds required taxes but does not provide tax advice.

9. CONCENTRATION RISK

9.1 Geographic Concentration

If you invest primarily in one location (e.g., Lagos), you're exposed to location-specific risks.

9.2 Property Type Concentration

Investing heavily in one property type (e.g., student housing) increases exposure to sector-specific risks.

9.3 Single Property Risk

Putting significant capital in one property eliminates diversification benefits.

10. SPECIFIC PROPERTY TYPE RISKS

10.1 Student Housing

  • • High seasonal vacancy (3-4 months annually)
  • • Dependent on university enrollment
  • • Higher wear and tear
  • • Academic calendar disruptions

10.2 Commercial Properties

  • • Economic sensitivity (businesses close during recessions)
  • • Longer vacancy periods when empty
  • • Tenant improvement costs
  • • Lease negotiation complexities

10.3 Residential Properties

  • • Regulatory risks (rent control)
  • • Tenant rights protections
  • • Maintenance demands
  • • Market saturation

11. FORCE MAJEURE RISKS

Events beyond our control can severely impact investments:

11.1 Natural Disasters

  • • Flooding
  • • Earthquakes
  • • Storms
  • • Environmental contamination

11.2 Pandemics

Health crises can cause:

  • • Economic shutdowns
  • • Tenant payment defaults
  • • Property access restrictions
  • • Market disruptions

11.3 Political Instability

  • • Civil unrest
  • • Government instability
  • • Policy unpredictability
  • • Security concerns

11.4 Infrastructure Failures

  • • Power grid failures
  • • Water supply disruptions
  • • Road deterioration
  • • Communication breakdowns

12. FINANCIAL PROJECTIONS AND ASSUMPTIONS

12.1 Projection Uncertainty

All financial projections are based on assumptions that may prove incorrect:

  • • Market rent estimates
  • • Occupancy rates
  • • Operating expense forecasts
  • • Property value appreciation
  • • Exit timing and pricing

12.2 Best-Case Scenarios

Projected returns often represent favorable scenarios. Actual results are typically lower.

12.3 Unforeseen Costs

Unexpected expenses can significantly reduce returns:

  • • Major repairs
  • • Legal fees
  • • Regulatory compliance costs
  • • Special assessments

13. LACK OF CONTROL

13.1 Limited Decision-Making

As fractional owner, you have limited control over:

  • • Property management decisions
  • • Tenant selection
  • • Maintenance priorities
  • • Sale timing
  • • Major improvements

13.2 Majority Rules

Major decisions may be made by majority vote. You may disagree with decisions but be bound by them.

14. CONFLICTS OF INTEREST

14.1 Platform Fees

BlockVest earns fees from your returns, which may create incentive misalignment.

14.2 Related Parties

BlockVest may have relationships with service providers (property managers, legal advisors), creating potential conflicts.

15. INFORMATION LIMITATIONS

15.1 Due Diligence Limitations

Despite thorough verification, we may not discover:

  • • Hidden property defects
  • • Undisclosed legal issues
  • • Inaccurate representations
  • • Future problems

15.2 Information Delays

Property performance information may be delayed by:

  • • Reporting timelines
  • • Management company schedules
  • • Third-party dependencies

16. NO INVESTMENT ADVICE

BlockVest does not provide investment, financial, tax, or legal advice. We provide information only.

You should:

  • • Conduct your own research
  • • Consult qualified advisors
  • • Understand your personal financial situation
  • • Make informed decisions independently

17. SUITABILITY

Real estate investing through BlockVest may not be suitable for:

  • • Those unable to afford potential losses
  • • Those unfamiliar with real estate risks
  • • Those requiring guaranteed income
  • • Those with low risk tolerance

Only invest money you can afford to lose and do not need for near-term expenses.
